Ready To Become a Blood Donor?

We've broken down the process into three simple phases: before, during, and after your donation. Follow these steps to ensure a smooth, rewarding, and life-saving experience.

Before Your Donation

Planning ahead makes all the difference! Here’s how to prepare:

  • Hydrate: Drink plenty of water or juice the day before and the day of your donation.
  • Eat Well: Have a good meal 2-3 hours before donating. Avoid fatty foods.
  • Rest: Get a good night’s sleep.
  • Bring ID: You must have a photo ID and know any medications you are taking.

During Your Donation

The entire process takes about an hour, but the actual collection is quick:

  • Health Screening: Complete a quick health check including pulse, blood pressure, and hemoglobin levels.
  • Relax: The donation itself takes 8–10 minutes for whole blood. Find a comfortable position and relax.
  • Refreshments: After collection, you'll be offered snacks and drinks to replenish fluids.

After Your Donation

Your body quickly begins replacing the donated blood. Follow these easy steps to feel great:

Snack & Stay Hydrated

Enjoy the refreshments and keep drinking liquids throughout the day.

Skip Strenuous Activity

Avoid heavy lifting or vigorous exercise for the next 24 hours.

Plan Your Next Gift

You can donate whole blood again in 56 days. We'll send a reminder!
