Blood Donation Basics

Giving blood is easier than you think. Learn the basic steps and see how simple it is to save a life.

1. Registration

When you arrive at the blood center or mobile bus, you will be asked to provide a photo ID and read some educational materials.

2. Health Screening

We check your temperature, pulse, blood pressure, and hemoglobin levels. This mini-physical ensures it is safe for you to give blood.

3. Donation

Sit back, relax, and save lives! The actual donation takes only 8-10 minutes for whole blood. Refreshments are provided afterwards.

About Blood

Blood has four main components: red cells, platelets, plasma, and white blood cells. Each part plays a vital role in saving lives.

Donation Methods

Whole Blood Donation

The most common type. You donate about a pint of whole blood. Ideally for all blood types.

Double Red Cell

Allows you to donate two units of red cells at once. Ideal for types O-, O+, A-, A+.

Platelet Donation

An automated process to collect only platelets. Vital for cancer patients.
