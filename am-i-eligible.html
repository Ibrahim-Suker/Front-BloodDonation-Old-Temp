Can I Donate Blood?

To maximize the life-saving potential of every unit and deliver the right product to the right patient, blood donation centers may need to target specific donation types based on your blood type and eligibility.

Whole Blood

The most common type of donation where about a pint of whole blood is collected.

  • Age and Weight: You must be 16 years old and weigh at least 110 lbs.
  • Timing: Can donate every 56 days.
  • Ideal for: All blood types. Used to help trauma patients, surgical patients, and those with chronic illnesses.

Double Red Cells

A specialized process (apheresis) where a machine collects two units of red blood cells while returning plasma and platelets to the donor.

  • Age and Weight: Must be 17 years old and meet specific height/weight requirements (e.g., males > 130 lbs, females > 150 lbs).
  • Timing: Can donate every 112 days.
  • Ideal for: Types O-, O+, A-, B- donors. Often used for accident victims and surgical patients.

Platelets

An automated process (apheresis) to collect only platelets and sometimes plasma, returning the red cells.

  • Age and Weight: Must be 17 years old and weigh at least 110 lbs.
  • Timing: Can donate every 7 days (up to 24 times a year).
  • Ideal for: Patients undergoing chemotherapy, organ transplants, and those with bleeding disorders.

Am I Eligible?

Understanding the basic requirements for blood donation.

Basic Donor Eligibility Requirements

To ensure the safety of both the donor and the patient, there are general criteria that must be met before donating blood.

Weight

Must weigh at least 50 kg (110 lbs).

Age

Generally 18 years or older (some locations accept 17 with parental consent).

Health

Must be in good general health and feel well on the day of donation.

Time Frame

At least 56 days must have passed since your last whole blood donation.

Read Full FAQs

Did I get a tattoo?

Tattoo deferral is usually one year unless done in a licensed sterile facility.

Did I recently travel?

Travel deferral depends on location; some high-risk areas require a waiting period.

Am I taking medication?

Most medications are fine, but some (like antibiotics) require a waiting period.

Can't Donate?

There are still many ways you can help save lives even if you aren't eligible to donate blood today.
